What You Need

You only need your imagination and a set of dice.


Dungeons and Dragons (D&D or DnD) is a tabletop role-playing game (RPG) that has been around since the 1970's. It was a fairly niche game, but became popularized in recent years by media such as the Netflix Show Stranger Things and the Web-show Critical Role. Both bringing Dungeons and Dragons to more popular media and the mainstream part of the internet.




Materials

Although it is technically possible to play DnD without dice, most Dungeon Masters and Players use them. Dice help to randomize events to help your story unfold in a more natural way that allows everyone to be a hero, and makes everyone overcome adversity from time to time.



You will need the rules of the game. You can find everything from how to create a character to a list of possible items and their values in the Players Handbook. For aspiring Dungeon Masters, the Dungeon Masters Guide is a must-have. You can start playing with only those two books, but you may want to branch out into other books that have more monsters, races, classes, lands, and encounters. A great online resource for this type of thing is DnD Beyond. There, you can create a character for free and access limited content from the Players Handbook.




If you want to see where your characters are in relation to the monsters they are fighting, you may want to invest in some miniatures.



A great recourse to create your own Miniature Figure is Hero Forge.
